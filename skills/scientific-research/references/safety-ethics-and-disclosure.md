# Safety, Ethics, And Disclosure

Use this reference when research may involve human subjects, clinical or medical claims, privacy, dual use, regulated domains, wet lab work, field deployment, private data, or publishable AI-assisted writing.

## Privacy And Secrets

- Do not inspect `.env`, credentials, private keys, tokens, password stores, or unrelated personal files.
- Use private datasets only when the user clearly provided them for the task.
- Minimize copying sensitive data into reports.
- Prefer aggregate or de-identified outputs.
- Flag re-identification risk.

## Human Subjects

Human-subjects work can include surveys, interviews, behavioral tracking, user studies, clinical data, educational interventions, and workplace monitoring.

Required checks:

- Consent.
- Recruitment fairness.
- Risk-benefit balance.
- Data retention and access control.
- Vulnerable populations.
- Compensation and coercion.
- Institutional review or ethics approval.

Without approval, provide protocol design and ethics review only.

## Medical And Clinical Claims

- Do not provide diagnosis, treatment instructions, or clinical implementation guidance.
- Use medical literature carefully and recommend qualified professional review.
- Distinguish research hypotheses from clinical advice.
- Require validation, regulatory, and ethics considerations for deployment claims.

## Dual Use And Safety-Sensitive Work

Flag and narrow work that could enable:

- Weaponization.
- Cyber abuse.
- Surveillance or privacy invasion.
- Biological, chemical, or physical harm.
- Deceptive manipulation.

Offer safer alternatives such as risk assessment, defensive evaluation, policy analysis, or high-level literature review.

## Wet Lab And Field Work

Do not provide operational wet-lab or field execution steps that enable unsafe activity. Keep outputs at:

- Literature review.
- Risk assessment.
- Ethics checklist.
- Non-operational protocol outline.
- Questions for qualified supervisors or review boards.

## AI-Use Disclosure

Include disclosure for publishable artifacts, research reports, manuscripts, grant drafts, peer-review simulations, or generated analyses.

Minimum disclosure elements:

- AI system assisted with the artifact.
- Type of assistance.
- Human responsibility for verification.
- Source, result, and compliance limitations.

Do not remove disclosure to make the work appear unaided.
